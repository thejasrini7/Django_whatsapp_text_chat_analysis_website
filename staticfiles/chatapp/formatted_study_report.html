5. Communication Style

The communication style within the group was primarily informational with distinct roles among participants:

  • Informational Broadcasts: Most messages were announcements rather than sustained discussions
  • Role-based Communication:
    • Sf Mangesh Baskar Sir acted as the primary source of information and guidance
    • Sf Kalpanjay Nathe facilitated communication about events and meetings
    • Other participants primarily acknowledged or provided brief feedback
  • Language Preference: The use of Marathi language was prevalent throughout the communications
  • Multimodal Communication: Used text, documents, images, and audio files for information sharing
  • Limited Interaction: Minimal back-and-forth conversation; most interactions were acknowledgments
Communication Insight: The group functions more as an information distribution channel than a discussion forum, with clear role differentiation among participants.

7. Recommendations

  1. Enhance Interactive Discussions: Encourage more back-and-forth conversations to create a more collaborative learning environment
  2. Regular Check-ins: Implement periodic question-and-answer sessions to address specific concerns of group members
  3. Diversify Content Sharing: Include more varied formats such as video tutorials or live demonstrations
